Burger Recipe

Description

To make a classic, juicy burger, start with high-quality ground beef—ideally 80/20 (80% lean, 20% fat) for the best flavor and juiciness. Form the beef into loose patties about ¾ inch thick, handling the meat as little as possible to keep it tender. Season both sides generously with salt and pepper. Grill or pan-sear the patties over medium-high heat for about 3–4 minutes per side for medium doneness, adding slices of cheese (like cheddar, American, or Swiss) during the last minute of cooking so it melts perfectly.

While the patties cook, toast your burger buns—brioche or potato buns work great—until lightly golden. To assemble, spread your favorite condiments like mayo, ketchup, or mustard on the bottom bun, then layer with lettuce, tomato slices, pickles, and the hot burger patty. Add more toppings like grilled onions, bacon, or avocado if desired, then top with the bun lid. Serve hot with fries or chips, and enjoy a perfectly balanced, flavorful burger.

Ingredients

  • Ground beef
  • Burger buns
  • Cheese slices
  • Lettuce
  • Tomato
  • Pickles
  • Red onion
  • Ketchup
  • Mustard
  • Mayonnaise
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Olive oil

Steps

  1. Prepare the Patties
  2. Heat the Grill or Pan
  3. Cook the Patties
  4. Toast the Buns
  5. Assemble the Burger
  6. Serve
